1.Introduction
1.1. Project Title
UWU-NEXUS: A Centralized Digital Ecosystem for Student Life Management.
1.2. Project Description
UWU-NEXUS is a comprehensive, university exclusive web application designed to
centralize and digitize the core aspects of student life at Uva Wellassa University
(UWU). The platform operates under a strict security constraint:
The ecosystem integrates six key functional modules into a single, cohesive platform,
each addressing a specific gap in current university digital infrastructure:
1. University Event Calendar - A centralized scheduling system that displays all
upcoming university activities and events, accessible by all registered students.
2. Event Ticket Booking - A digital ticketing platform that simplifies the process
of organizing and attending paid events on campus, allowing clubs to sell tickets
and students to purchase them online.
3. Sarasawi Alewisala (Online Marketplace) - A digital transformation of the
traditional campus marketplace, providing a dedicated student to student
e - commerce platform within the trusted UWU network.
4. Lost and Found - A structured portal for students to report misplaced
belongings and locate items discovered on campus, replacing the current
informal, untracked process.
5. Smart GPA Calculator - An intelligent academic tool that automatically
identifies a student's degree program and loads their specific curriculum for
accurate GPA computation.
6. Information Hub - A comprehensive digital directory providing verified
information on university procedures, lecturer contacts and emergency hotlines.

1.3. Background and Motivation
As Uva Wellassa University (UWU) continues to evolve as a center of academic
excellence, the digital integration of its diverse campus activities presents a significant
opportunity to further enhance the student experience. While current communication
methods reflect a vibrant and active student body, there is a growing potential to
modernize these interactions through a unified digital framework. Research
consistently highlights that implementing integrated digital ecosystems can
significantly optimize engagement, coordination, and administrative flow within
university environments.
Currently, university events are advertised through various social media groups and
physical boards. Implementing a centralized management system offers a proactive way
to minimize scheduling conflicts and ensure the student community remains
consistently informed and engaged.
In the area of campus commerce, the traditional 'Sarasawi Alewisala' marketplace
provides a strong foundation for student entrepreneurship. Enhancing this with a
dedicated digital infrastructure would provide verified records and a more secure,
structured environment for student-to-student transactions. Similarly, formalizing the
lost and found process through a structured digital portal can improve recovery rates
for personal belongings beyond what is currently possible through informal social
media reports.
Academic tracking also presents an opportunity for modernization. Transitioning from
manual calculations to automated GPA monitoring tools can enhance academic
awareness and help students engage more effectively with their degree progression.
Furthermore, a unified repository would assist students, particularly those in their first
year, in navigating university procedures and locating verified lecturer contacts more
efficiently. This support reduces early-semester stress and facilitates a smoother
transition into university life.
These opportunities inspired UWU-NEXUS, a digital ecosystem designed to enhance
campus life. As Computer Science and Technology students at Uva Wellassa
University of Sri Lanka, we aim to create an innovative solution that supports our peers.

1.4. Problem in Brief
The student community at Uva Wellassa University currently operates without a unified
digital platform to manage campus activities, academic tools, and peer-to-peer
interactions. A detailed analysis of the current situation reveals the following critical
problems:
1. Event Scheduling Conflicts: Clubs and academic departments schedule events
independently with no awareness of each other's calendars, frequently resulting
in date clashes, reduced student attendance, and wasted organizational effort.
2. Inefficient Ticket Management and Accessibility Issues: Current paid
campus events rely on manual ticket distribution and cash payments, making it
difficult and time-consuming for students to purchase tickets. This leads to
errors, lost payments, undocumented transactions, and an overall poor
experience for both organizers and attendees.
3. Absence of a Trusted Student Marketplace: There is no secure, universityendorsed digital platform for students to buy and sell goods or services.
Commerce occurs through informal channels without verification, creating risks
for both buyers and sellers.
4. Unstructured Lost and Found Process: Students who lose personal items
have no formal reporting mechanism. Found items are posted informally with
no central tracking system, resulting in very low recovery rates and widespread
frustration.
5. Manual and Tedious GPA Calculation: While general GPA tools exist online,
manually entering credit values for each individual subject is difficult and timeconsuming. This manual process also increases the risk of calculation errors,
leading to inaccurate results.
6. Critical Information Inaccessibility: New and returning students alike
struggle to find accurate, up-to-date information on university procedures,
lecturer contact details, campus layout, and emergency contacts. The lack of a
central information repository contributes to administrative confusion and
unnecessary delays.
4
1.5. Proposed Solution
UWU-NEXUS is an integrated, six-module web platform addressing identified
problems through a secure, university email-authenticated environment. The solution
for each area is detailed below:
1. University Event Calendar: Clubs and academic departments will publish
events onto a shared digital calendar. This allows organizers to check for
overlaps while students stay informed of upcoming campus activities.
2. Event Ticket Booking System: Allows student organizations to create events
with defined prices and deadlines. Students securely purchase tickets online,
eliminating the need for manual cash handling and physical distribution.
3. Sarasawi Alewisala - Online Marketplace: A dedicated e-commerce module
for student entrepreneurs to list products or services. Verified university access
ensures a trusted network for buyers and sellers to interact directly.
4. Lost and Found Portal: A structured registry for reporting lost or found items.
The system facilitates community-driven recovery within a verified university
network using detailed reports and contact information.
5. Smart GPA Calculator: Automatically identifies a student's degree program
via their university email index to load specific curricula. Students enter grades
for instant GPA calculation and degree classification.
6. Information Hub: A comprehensive directory covering university procedures,
lecturer profiles and emergency hotlines. This serves as the primary point of
reference for all students.

1.6. Project Aim and Objectives
1.6.1. Project Aim
The aim of this project is to design and develop UWU-NEXUS, a secure, scalable, and
user friendly web application that centralizes student services, event management,
academic tools, and university information into a single authenticated platform,
exclusively accessible to the verified student and staff community of Uva Wellassa
University of Sri Lanka.
1.6.2. Project Objectives
1. To implement a University Event Calendar, enabling clubs and departments
to coordinate events without scheduling clashes and allowing students to
discover and follow campus activities with ease.
2. To build a fully functional digital Event Ticket Booking module that
automates online sales and booking confirmation.
3. To create the Sarasawi Alewisala Online Marketplace, providing students
with a trusted, structured digital platform to list, browse, and purchase goods
and services within the verified UWU network.
4. To develop a Lost and Found portal with structured reporting forms, enabling
students to efficiently report and recover misplaced personal belongings through
community participation.
5. To implement a Smart GPA Calculator that automatically detects a student's
degree program via their email index format, loads the appropriate curriculum,
and computes the GPA and degree classification.
6. To construct a comprehensive Information Hub containing university
procedures, complete lecturer profiles and emergency contacts to serve as a
reliable first point of reference for all students.

2. Methodology
2.1. Waterfall Model Methodology
Figure 1 : Waterfall Software Development Methodology
The development of UWU-NEXUS will strictly follow the Waterfall Methodology.
This sequential, phase-based model is well-suited to this project because the system
requirements are clearly defined from the outset and the scope is well-bounded within
the constraints of a 200-level university project. The Waterfall model ensures that each
phase is fully completed and verified before progressing to the next, providing a
structured and disciplined development process.
The following six phases outline the complete development lifecycle:
1. Phase 1 - Requirements Analysis: The team will identify and document all
functional and non-functional requirements. We will conduct observations of
current campus processes and interview students and club administrators to
capture daily pain points. The output is a comprehensive Software
Requirements Specification (SRS) defining the system’s performance and
functionality.
7
2. Phase 2 - System Design: The system architecture will be designed, including
the MySQL database schema, the Next.js component hierarchy for the
frontend, and the PHP module architecture for the backend. Key UML diagrams
(Use Case, ER, Class, and Data Flow) and the User Interface (UI) wireframes
for all six modules will be finalized.
3. Phase 3 - Implementation: The system will be developed module by module.
The frontend will be built from scratch using Next.js, and the backend will be
implemented using hard-coded PHP without frameworks, using MySQL for
data management. Development will follow a specific order: authentication,
calendar, ticketing, marketplace, lost and found, GPA calculator, and
information hub.
4. Phase 4 - Testing: Comprehensive testing will verify that each module meets
its requirements. This includes Unit testing for individual features, Integration
testing for module interaction, and User Acceptance Testing (UAT) with
student volunteers. The @uwu.ac.lk email authentication will be rigorously
tested for security.
5. Phase 5 - Deployment: Upon successful testing, the system will be deployed
to a local or institutional server environment to simulate real-world usage within
the university setting. Technical configurations will be finalized to ensure the
platform is stable and accessible for the student community.
6. Phase 6 - Maintenance: Following deployment, the system will be monitored
for performance issues, security vulnerabilities, and bugs. Corrective
maintenance will be performed based on user feedback. Potential future
enhancements—such as push notification support and integration with the
university's official student portal—will be documented for future
consideration.

3. Requirement Identification
3.1. Functional and Non-Functional Requirements
3.1.1. Functional Requirements
1. User Access & Identification
• The system shall be an open-access platform for the university community
without requiring a formal registration or login process.
• The system shall require a valid @uwu.ac.lk university email or index number
only for specific functional interactions.(e.g., ticket booking or GPA
calculation).
2. University Event Calendar
• Club and academic department administrators shall be able to create events
by specifying a title, description, date, time, and venue.
• Administrators shall be able to update or cancel their own events as needed.
3. Event Ticket Booking System
• Only the System Administrator shall be able to create ticketed events with
defined prices and available quantities.
• Students shall provide their university email to purchase tickets through the
platform.
• The system shall send a unique digital booking confirmation directly to the
provided university email address for each successful transaction.
• The system shall automatically stop ticket sales once the event is sold out or the
deadline has passed.
9
4. Sarasawi Alewisala - Online Marketplace
• Students shall be able to create marketplace listings by providing an item name,
description, price, condition, photos, and a contact email.
• The system shall allow buyers to contact sellers directly via the contact details
provided in the listing.
• Sellers shall be able to remove their listings once an item is sold.
5. Lost and Found Portal
• Students shall be able to submit reports for lost or found items, including
descriptions, last known locations, photos, and contact information.
• The system shall facilitate direct communication between parties using the
contact details provided in the reports.
• Once an item is recovered, the reporter shall be able to remove the report from
the active list.
6. Smart GPA Calculator
• The system shall allow students to enter their university email/index to
automatically identify their degree program.
• The platform shall pre-load the relevant curriculum, including subjects and
credit values, based on the identified program.
• Students shall enter their grades to receive an accurate Weighted GPA and
degree classification instantly.
7. Information Hub
• Provide structured information on university procedures. (medical submissions,
registration, etc.)
• Maintain a searchable directory of lecturers and contacts.
• Emergency contact numbers and campus hotlines shall be prominently
displayed.
• The System Administrator shall have the authority to update all Information
Hub content.
10
3.1.2. Non-Functional Requirements
1. Security
• All data transmission between the client and server shall be encrypted using
HTTPS.
• Administrator passwords shall be hashed and salted before storage; plain text
passwords shall never be stored.
2. Performance
• The system shall support concurrent access by multiple users without
measurable performance degradation.
• The GPA Calculator shall produce results in real time with no perceptible delay.
3. Scalability
• The database schema shall be designed to accommodate growth in the number
of users, events, and marketplace listings without requiring architectural
changes.
• The modular design of the application shall allow new features or modules to
be added in the future without disrupting existing functionality.
4. Usability
• The interface shall be intuitive and consistent across all modules, requiring no
prior technical training for students to use effectively.
• The system shall display clear, actionable error messages when users submit
invalid input.
• The system shall be fully responsive and usable across modern desktop and
mobile web browsers.
5. Availability and Reliability
• All data shall be stored persistently in the MySQL database, ensuring data
integrity across sessions.
• Scheduled maintenance periods shall be communicated to users at least 24 hours
in advance.

3.2. System Requirements
3.2.1. Hardware Requirements
1. Development Machines: Laptop or desktop computer with a minimum of 4
GB RAM, Dual Core Processor or higher, and 500 GB HDD or higher.
2. Operating System: Linux/Windows 10 / Windows 11.
3. Network: Stable internet connection via Wi-Fi or Ethernet for development,
testing, and deployment.
3.2.2. Software Requirements
1. Front End Framework: Next.js - all components and pages to be built
entirely from scratch without pre built UI templates.
2. Back End Language: PHP. (plain, hard coded; no frameworks such as
Laravel or CodeIgniter to be used).
3. Database Management System: MySQL - used for all relational data
storage.
4. Local Development Server: Apache via XAMPP. (for local PHP and
MySQL development environment).
5. Code Editor: Visual Studio Code with relevant extensions. (PHP
Intelephense, ESLint, Prettier)
6. Version Control: Git with GitHub for source code management and team
collaboration.
7. Web Browser: Google Chrome (primary testing); Mozilla Firefox and
Microsoft Edge (cross browser validation).

3.3. User Roles
The UWU-NEXUS platform supports three distinct user roles, each with specific
permissions and access levels:
1. Student (General User)
• Can browse and follow events on the University Event Calendar.
• Can purchase tickets for campus events.
• Can list products, browse listings, and contact sellers in the Sarasawi Alewisala
Marketplace.
• Can submit and view lost and found reports.
• Can use the Smart GPA Calculator with their auto-detected degree curriculum.
• Can access all content within the Information Hub.
2. Club / Department Administrator
• Can create, edit, and delete events on the University Event Calendar.
• Can request the System Administrator to add or manage tickets for their events.
3. System Administrator
• Has full administrative control over all platform modules.
• The only role authorized to set up and manage ticketed events, including pricing
and quantities.
• Can remove inappropriate marketplace listings or lost/found posts.
• Can create, update, and delete all content in the Information Hub.
• Responsible for monitoring system-wide activity and maintaining information
accuracy.
